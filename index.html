Solace: Project Overview

Trailer Video

Project Goals

I wanted to create an RPG that gave the player a wide range of abilities without forcing them to choose between a few and still be executable within a few button presses. Inspired by Final Fantasy 14, I used trigger buttons as gates for ability usage, enabling more abilities with minimal button clutter.

Another goal was to design enemies that worked together, using timed attacks and formations. Ground telegraphs created engaging scenarios, requiring the player to solve puzzles in combat and align with level design.

Elemental Stances

Players can switch between Fire, Water, Lightning, and Wind stances to enhance strategy and combat choices:

  • Fire: High single target damage
  • Water: Crowd control
  • Wind: Area of effect attacks
  • Lightning: Ranged abilities for distant foes

Critical Hits

Critical hits in Solace aren't random. Instead, they're triggered by ability synergies. For instance, applying the Burn status and following up with a Water attack creates an explosion for a critical hit. Critical hits throw enemies off balance, with bold text feedback for players.

Hot Bar

The Hot Bar allows quick access to abilities segmented by gamepad triggers for intuitive gameplay. Originally designed with 16 buttons, playtesting led to streamlining it to 8 buttons to reduce clutter while maintaining functionality.

Teleportation System

Redesigned for a player-centric approach, the teleportation system provides dynamic mobility. Players can instantly move to specific locations, enhancing exploration and dodging mechanics. It also includes a unique parry mechanic called Teleport Strike.

Enemy Formation System

Enemies in Solace work together with synchronized attacks, requiring players to think strategically. Attack telegraphs and coordinated formations create engaging combat puzzles that challenge player response and positioning.
